## Priority 1 — Frederick Kuthe (German origins + Dent County, MO)

### What I can support online (with evidence)

**Surname origin / region clues**

* Kuthe / Küthe / Kuethe is a German surname; one documented etymology (via Dictionary of American Family Names) treats Kuethe (Küthe) as a German topographic name from Kute/Kaute ("quarry / pit / depression").
* Forebears' distribution for Kuthe is messy globally (strongly India-skewed), but it does show some presence in Germany and the US—useful mainly as a clue that spelling variants matter.

**A specific "Germany origin" claim exists, but it's not primary**

* Geni claims Frederick was from Darmstadt, Hesse, Germany, and that he was husband of Sarah Stegall and father of George Washington Kuthe. Treat this as a hint, not proof.

### What you should target next (best odds of real proof)

To answer "where in Germany was he from" you usually need one of these:

1. Naturalization record (county/state court; often gives birthplace or at least "Germany/Prussia/Bavaria")
2. Passenger list (esp. if arrival is late enough to be recorded well)
3. Civil death record / obituary naming birthplace (rare in rural MO, but sometimes)

Highest-value searches for Frederick in Dent County, MO:

* 1870 and 1880 US Census (Dent County): look for Frederick / Fritz / Fred + surname variants Kuthe / Kuethe / Kuehte / Küthe / Cooty (wild mistranscriptions happen).
* Dent County marriage books (for Frederick + Sarah Ann Steagall)
* Dent County probate / estate (if he died before George left home, probate can name heirs)

**One important conflict to resolve:**

* Dent County marriage index pages show an entry that looks like Sarah Ann Kuthe marrying (someone) Patterson in Nov 1867. That could mean:
  * your Sarah Ann (Steagall) is not the same person, or
  * she was widowed/divorced, or
  * the index is a different Sarah Ann Kuthe entirely.
