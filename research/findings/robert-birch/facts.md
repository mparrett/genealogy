## Business Ventures

### Prescott Years - Saloon Business
**Business:** "Birch Bros." saloon in Prescott
**Partners:** Robert and brother William "Slim" Birch
**Timeframe:** By 1902 through January 1, 1915
**End Date:** Arizona statewide Prohibition began January 1, 1915

**Source:** Sharlot Hall Museum; William Birch obituary (1952)

### Agua Caliente Hot Springs Resort

**Resort Details:**
- 22-room hotel built around natural hot springs
- Location: Maricopa County, Arizona (Gila River desert)
- Built: 1897 (originally built to cater to railroad passengers)
- Distance: 12-14 miles from Sentinel depot, 1-2 miles from Hyder depot

**Robert's Acquisition/Management:**
- **Began Operating:** Mid-1910s (exact date unknown, likely after 1915 Prohibition)
- **Partnership:** Jointly operated with brother William "Slim" Birch
  - Robert: On-site manager and host
  - William: Logistics from Prescott, possibly financial backing
- **Listed Operator:** 1938 Arizona State Hotel Directory: "Agua Caliente Hotel (Hot Springs) â€“ Robert Birch"

**Resort Operations:**
- Provided mineral baths, lodging, dining
- Catered to railroad workers, health seekers, travelers, Arizona politicians
- Family lived on-site (at least seasonally)
- Operated through Great Depression
- Closed early 1940s (WWII era)

**Source:** Arizona State Hotel Directory 1938; Sharlot Hall Museum (William Birch obituary); ChatGPT research (verified); historical accounts

## Verified Timeline

- **May 23, 1869:** Birth in Ottawa, Ontario, Canada
- **May 23, 1894:** Marriage to Sarah Frances Cope (age 25)
- **Late 1890s:** Daughter Mary born
- **By 1902:** Established in Prescott, Arizona Territory with brother William
- **By 1906:** Parents Joseph & Frances living in Prescott
- **1902-1915:** Operated "Birch Bros." saloon with brother William
- **January 1, 1915:** Arizona Prohibition begins (saloon closes)
- **Mid-1910s:** Began operating Agua Caliente Hot Springs (exact date unknown)
- **March 20, 1927:** Governor Hunt visited; photographed with Mary Birch and Doris Higgins
- **~1928:** Took in infant Howard Higgins
- **April 6, 1930:** Daughter Mary married Harold Carpenter at the resort
- **1930s:** Continued operating resort through Great Depression
- **1938:** Listed as proprietor in Arizona State Hotel Directory
- **Early 1940s:** Resort closed (WWII era, springs drying up)
- **By 1950:** Living in Phoenix with/near daughter Mary's family
- **August 6, 1950:** Death in Phoenix (age 81)
- **Burial:** Mountain View Cemetery, Prescott (Birch family mausoleum)
